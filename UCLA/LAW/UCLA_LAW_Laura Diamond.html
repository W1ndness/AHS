Laura Diamond

Lecturer in Law

  • Home Page
  • Faculty & Research
  • Faculty Profiles
  • Laura Diamond

Laura Diamond is a Supervising Attorney in the UCLA Immigrant Family Law Clinic. During the Spring 2022 semester, she is co-leading a special project focused on providing legal support to Afghan evacuees, and supervising law students in providing pro bono representation to Afghan asylum-seekers.

Diamond previously was a Senior Staff Attorney with Center for Human Rights and Constitutional Law, where she participated in defending the Flores Consent Decree to protect the rights of unaccompanied immigrant youth detained by the U.S. government, and represented individual immigrant youth in their path to legal status. As a pro bono immigration attorney, Diamond obtained the release of detained immigrants from Adelanto Detention Center, and won grants of asylum for Central Americans fleeing violence. In 2021, Esperanza Immigrant Rights Project recognized Diamond for her pro bono work.

Diamond received her B.A. cum laude from the University of Pennsylvania, and her J.D. from UC Berkeley School of Law. Diamond clerked for (Ret.) Hon. George H. King on the U.S. District Court for the Central District of California.

Before pivoting to immigration law, Diamond practiced civil rights impact litigation, including disability rights, educational equity, environmental justice, and consumer rights with leading non-profit law firms in Los Angeles.

In addition to her legal practice and teaching, Diamond is the author and/or editor of three books and writes a weekly blog.
